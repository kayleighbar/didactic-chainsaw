Hailing from Scotland, SOPHIE (September 17, 1986 - January 30, 2021) was an influential electronic music producer and artist, working in a similar vain of the PC Music crowd. She worked with notable acts such as Madonna, Charli XCX, and Vince Staples.


In 2018, Sophie released her debut album OIL OF EVERY PEARL'S UN-INSIDES, which brought her critical acclaim and attention of music giants of the likes of Pitchfork which dubbed the album sprawling and beautiful, while still keeping the disorienting, latex-pop feel of her fascinating production technique. It was named "Best New Music" by the critical magazine with a score of 8.6. She was also nominated for a Grammy for the same album.


It wasn't until recently when Sophie actually became the face of work. When she first started making music, she was seldom seen. She did not care to be the face of her music, but rather let the work speak for itself. Her compilation album PRODUCT focused around creating sounds for objects that did not exist. With these albums she bundled a bunch of different "products" including a puffer jacket, platform shoes, and a sex toy.
SOPHIE first stars in the music video for her emotionally raw and vulnerable song "It's Okay to Cry" shortly after coming out as a transgender woman and then becoming the face of her music videos. In the video, SOPHIE appears naked and is emotionally singing and dancing to the song with clouds behind her.
